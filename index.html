How To Coffee

This is a step-by-step guide on how Jake makes coffee using the french press.

1:Fill The Kettle

  • Fill to the Max Fill line inside the Kettle.
  • Use the best water avaliable.
  • Press both the On/Off and the Hold Button.
  • This should take about 5 Minutes.

2:Weigh and Grind Beans

  • The Ratio is "1 gram coffee to 17 grams water".
  • As of 2/13/2022: Jake has been using ~42 grams of coffee.
  • Weigh on the scale in a bowl. Make sure to zero out the scale with the empty bowl on it before you weigh the beans.
  • Turn on the grinder first and then dump in the bean. This improves the life of the burrs...or something.

3:Start Brewing

  • Add ground coffee to the empty french press.
  • The water should be boiling or about 210 degrees Fahrentheit. The brew temp you want is between 205 and 195.
  • At the ratio of 1 to 17: 42 grams of coffee should have 731 grams of water.

4: Stop Brewing

  • Brewing will take ~4 Minutes.
  • When four minutes have elapsed. Stir the top several time. It doens't have to be excessive. Just send the grounds to the bottom.
  • Skim the foam at the top as best you can/feel like that day.

5:Let it sit.

  • Let the coffee sit for another 4 minutes or so.
  • This allows the smaller grounds to sink to the bottom.
  • It is unclear how much the step matters.
  • I usually just go until the milk frother stops.

6:Pour

  • James Hoffman says no press, and just sort of strains it with the lid part.
  • I press because why not. I don't think it affects too many fines.
  • I typically pour Jake's cup first. Holly's current mug is slightly smaller and therefore should look like it has a little more in it.
  • Top with frothed milk and serve.
